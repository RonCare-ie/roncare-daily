Who Cares? What carers in Ireland are actually entitled to — and why so many never see it.

Nearly 500,000 people in Ireland are family carers. Fewer than 100,000 receive Carer's Allowance. That gap is not an accident.

People in Ireland struggle to secure carer benefits due to fragmented information, strict means-testing, and complex PRSI (Pay Related Social Insurance) contribution conditions. Navigating the system is notoriously challenging — and the cost of getting it wrong falls entirely on the people already carrying the heaviest load.

Nearly 500,000 people in Ireland are family carers. They provide an average of 38.7 hours of unpaid care per week — collectively, around 19 million hours weekly. The annual replacement cost of that care is estimated at €20 billion. Yet fewer than 100,000 people receive Carer's Allowance. That gap is not an accident. It is the system working exactly as designed.

Carer's Allowance is a means-tested weekly payment for people caring for someone due to age, disability, or illness. To qualify, you must be providing full-time care (at least 35 hours per week), living with or near the person you care for, and your household income must fall below a set threshold. From July 2026, the weekly income disregard increases to €1,000 for a single person and €2,000 for a couple. If you qualify, you also receive a GP visit card, Free Travel, the Household Benefits Package, and the annual Carer's Support Grant.

Carer's Benefit is a PRSI-based payment for people who leave work or reduce their hours to care — available for up to 104 weeks. Carer's Support Grant is a once-yearly payment of €2,000 (increased from €1,850 in June 2025). Many carers don't know it exists — and if you don't qualify for Allowance or Benefit, you can still apply for the Grant separately.

The system fails carers in predictable ways: the means test punishes partnership, the 18.5-hour work cap is a poverty trap, and administrative burden is its own crisis. Family Carers Ireland has called for Carer's Allowance to be replaced by a Participatory Income — a payment that recognises informal care as a social contribution. In the meantime, contact Family Carers Ireland before attempting to navigate the system alone. If your application is delayed, apply for Supplementary Welfare Allowance at your local Intreo office while you wait. You are not just a carer. You are holding a significant part of this country's healthcare system together.
