From hospital to home — the shift that has to happen

The way we deliver healthcare is changing. Not as a preference — as a necessity.

Ireland's hospitals are under pressure that will not ease on its own. An ageing population, a nursing workforce stretched beyond capacity, and a chronic disease burden that grows every year. The system, as designed, cannot absorb what's coming. Something has to shift.

Originally introduced by Martin Curley as CIO of Ireland's HSE, Stay Left, Shift Left is a framework for reorienting healthcare away from hospitals and towards the person, the community, and the home. Stay Left is about prevention. Shift Left is about decentralisation — moving patients out of acute settings and back to community or home-based care as quickly and safely as possible.

It is being built right now. Ireland's Enhanced Community Care Programme assessed over 7,000 patients via mobile X-ray in 2024, with 95% treated at home. Virtual wards — live at St. Vincent's, University Hospital Limerick, Letterkenny, and more — have generated over 18,000 virtual bed days since mid-2024. In Letterkenny, a respiratory virtual ward produced an 18% reduction in acute admissions in 2025 alone.

If the plan is for more care to happen at home, the home needs to be ready to receive it. That means asking harder questions about who is in the home — monitoring, supporting, coordinating, and absorbing the care burden that used to sit inside a hospital ward. In many cases, the answer is a family member. Often a woman. Often without formal training, financial support, or recognition. Stay Left, Shift Left is a sound framework. But its success depends on the human infrastructure around it. Carers need support, not just patients.
