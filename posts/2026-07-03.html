Ireland Implements Historic July 2026 Carer Support Changes with €1,000 Weekly Income Disregard for Single Carers

Landmark reforms dramatically expand Carer's Allowance eligibility, ending years of income-based exclusions for working family carers.

There are an estimated 624,000 family carers in Ireland — people looking after a parent, a child with additional needs, or a partner with a long-term illness, often without a wage or a break. This week, two pieces of news brought that reality into sharp focus. Family Carers Ireland's State of Caring 2026 report found that 71% of carers are struggling to make ends meet, with almost half experiencing severe loneliness. And as of 2 July 2026, the Government rolled out the largest-ever increase to the Carer's Allowance means test — changes that could put real money back into the pockets of thousands of families who were previously locked out of support.

So what is Carer's Allowance, and how much can you get? The current rate is €270 per week for carers aged under 66, and €308 for those aged 66 and over. From 2 July 2026, the weekly income disregard jumped to €1,000 for a single carer and €2,000 for a couple — meaning a two-adult household earning up to roughly €110,000 a year can still receive the full payment.

If you were refused Carer's Allowance before because your income was too high, it's worth checking again right now. You can apply online through MyWelfare.ie or get help from Citizens Information on 0761 07 4000.

On top of Carer's Allowance, there is a separate annual payment called the Carer's Support Grant. Many carers in Ireland don't realise they may be entitled to it. In 2026, it is €2,000 per person you care for, paid in a lump sum each June. It is not means-tested, it is fully exempt from income tax, PRSI and USC, and you can spend it however you like.

If you are already receiving Carer's Allowance, Carer's Benefit or Domiciliary Care Allowance, you get it automatically — no form needed. If you are not on any of those payments but you are providing full-time care for at least six months, you can still apply using form CSG1 from the Department of Social Protection. If you care for more than one person, you receive one grant for each of them.

Carers on Carer's Allowance also qualify for a free GP Visit Card, the Household Benefits Package, and a Free Travel Pass. The first €50,000 of savings or other assets you hold (excluding your home) is not counted in the means test at all — something many Irish carers ask about when they worry their savings will disqualify them. If you are also getting another social welfare payment, such as a State Pension, you may be able to get Carer's Allowance at half-rate on top of it. For a full breakdown of what you are entitled to, Citizens Information at citizensinformation.ie is your best starting point.

If you are caring for someone at home and you have never applied for Carer's Allowance — or you were turned down before July 2026 — ring Citizens Information on 0761 07 4000 and ask them to look at your situation in light of the new income limits. You do not have to figure this out alone, and the rules have just changed in your favour. You are already doing one of the hardest jobs there is. The least the system can do is meet you halfway.
