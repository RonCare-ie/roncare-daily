Support & Community for Family Carers

Resources, guidance, and solidarity for those caring for a loved one across Europe

If you are currently caring for a loved one in Ireland or the UK, you are far from alone — and the very real toll it takes on your wellbeing is increasingly being recognised. Research and advocacy groups confirm that unpaid family carers face severe financial strain, exhaustion, and isolation: in Ireland, nearly 70% of carers face financial hardship and over 72% have never received respite care, while in Northern Ireland, two-thirds of unpaid carers report suffering mental ill-health as a direct result of their caring role. On the financial support side, there is genuinely helpful news right now: in Ireland, the Carer's Support Grant for 2026 is €2,000 (paid each June, tax-free, and spendable however you wish), and from July 2026, the income means test for Carer's Allowance is becoming more generous — raising the weekly income disregard from €625 to €1,000 for a single person and from €1,250 to €2,000 for a couple. In the UK, Carer's Allowance increased from £83.30 to £86.45 per week from 6 April 2026, and the earnings threshold has risen to £204 per week, meaning some carers who were previously ineligible may now qualify — it is worth checking your entitlement urgently. Respite care, though often hard to access, is a proven way to protect your mental and physical health, and organisations like Family Carers Ireland (www.familycarers.ie) and Carers UK (www.carersuk.org, helpline: 0808 808 7777) offer free guidance, advocacy, and peer community support. If you are struggling, please reach out — you deserve the same care and attention you so generously give to others.
