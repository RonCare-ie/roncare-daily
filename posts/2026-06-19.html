Support & Community for Family Carers

Resources, guidance, and solidarity for those caring for a loved one across Europe

Ireland's 624,000 family carers were in the spotlight this month during National Carers Week 2026 (June 8–14), with a simple but powerful theme: 'Thank you for caring.' The timing could not be more urgent. Family Carers Ireland's State of Caring 2026 report — published this week and based on nearly 3,000 carers' responses — found that 71% are struggling to make ends meet, while 48% experience severe loneliness. Almost three-quarters of those carers provide over 90 hours of unpaid care every single week, yet 75% have never received formal respite support. If you are one of those people quietly holding everything together at home, this article is for you.

The most practical financial support to know about right now is Carer's Allowance, which pays up to €270 per week. It is a means-tested payment from the Department of Social Protection, but a landmark change arrives on 2 July 2026: the weekly income disregard rises from €625 to €1,000 for a single carer, and from €1,250 to €2,000 for couples. Many carers who were previously told they earned too much to qualify should check again — you may now be eligible.

You can apply through MyWelfare.ie using a verified MyGovID account, or call Citizens Information on 0761 07 4000 for a free assessment of your situation before you apply.

On top of Carer's Allowance, there is the Carer's Support Grant — an annual, tax-free lump sum of €2,000 paid each June, with no means test attached. Over 147,000 carers received it automatically on 4 June 2026 this year if they were already on Carer's Allowance, Carer's Benefit, or Domiciliary Care Allowance. If you missed it, you can still apply before 31 December 2026 by submitting form CSG1. If you care for more than one person, you receive a separate €2,000 for each. Spend it on whatever you need most: a break, a bill, a piece of equipment.

If keeping your loved one at home matters most to you, the HSE Home Support Service is completely free to access. You do not need a medical card or means test to qualify. It is available to people aged 65 and over who need support at home, and the HSE also makes exceptions for younger people with conditions like early-onset dementia.

You apply by completing a Home Support application form and returning it to your local HSE Home Support Office — your GP, public health nurse, or a family member can help with this. Budget 2026 committed to funding 1.7 million more home support hours nationwide, so if you are on a waiting list, it is worth following up. Start at hse.ie/homesupportservice or call HSE Live free on 1800 700 700.

You are carrying something enormous, often without being asked if you need help. The one thing you could do today — right now — is call Citizens Information on 0761 07 4000 and ask them to check whether you qualify for Carer's Allowance under the new July 2026 rules, or whether you are missing any grant you are entitled to. It is a free call, they are genuinely helpful, and the answer could make a real difference to your week.
