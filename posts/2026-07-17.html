Ireland Implements Largest-Ever Income Disregard Increase for Family Carers, Allowing Single Carers to Earn €1,000 Weekly

Historic changes to Carer's Allowance and Carer's Benefit come into effect, enabling thousands of working families to access support previously denied on income grounds.

There are 624,000 family carers across Ireland, according to Family Carers Ireland — people who have put their own lives on hold to look after a parent, a child, or a partner who needs full-time support. This week, that reality came into sharp focus again: Family Carers Ireland published its pre-budget submission on 8 July 2026, calling for weekly carer payments to rise to €325 and for the means test to be scrapped entirely. The call comes just days after a landmark change took effect on 2 July 2026, when the Department of Social Protection introduced the largest-ever increase to the Carer's Allowance income disregards — a change that could mean thousands of carers who previously missed out on support can now qualify for the first time.

So what does Carer's Allowance actually pay, and who can get it? As of January 2026, the full rate is €270 per week if you are under 66, and €308 per week if you are 66 or over. If you care for two or more people, those figures rise to €405 and €462 respectively.

The payment is means-tested, but the big news from 2 July 2026 is that the weekly income disregard — the amount of earnings that is ignored in that means test — jumped from €625 to €1,000 for a single person, and from €1,250 to €2,000 for a couple. In plain terms, that means a single carer can now earn up to €50,000 a year and still receive the full payment, while a two-adult household earning around €100,000 can also retain it in full.

On top of Carer's Allowance, there is the Carer's Support Grant — a once-off annual payment of €2,000, which was paid out to over 147,000 carers on 4 June 2026. You get it automatically if you are on Carer's Allowance, but it is also available to carers who do not qualify for the allowance itself. You can apply online at MyWelfare.ie, or ring Citizens Information on 0761 07 4000 for help working out what you might be entitled to.

If you are wondering about getting extra help in the home — not just a payment, but actual hands-on support — the HSE Home Support Service (previously called the Home Care Package scheme) is worth knowing about. It provides funded care hours to help your loved one with personal care, getting in and out of bed, meals, and mobility. The service is free and not means-tested, so income is not assessed and you do not need a medical card.

The service is mainly for people aged 65 and over, but the HSE does make exceptions for people under 65 with early-onset dementia or a disability. To apply, you fill in the Home Support Service application form, available from your local HSE Home Support Office or at hse.ie/homesupportservice, and return it for a Care Needs Assessment. One important thing to know: unlike nursing home support under the Fair Deal Scheme, there is currently no legal right to home support in Ireland — access depends on your assessed needs and available resources in your area. In June 2026, the Health (Amendment) (Home Support Providers) Bill was passed, which will introduce formal regulation of home support providers by HIQA — a step forward for consistency and quality across the country.

If you are caring for someone right now and are not sure what you are entitled to, ring Citizens Information on 0761 07 4000. They can walk you through Carer's Allowance, the Carer's Support Grant, and the HSE Home Support Service based on your own situation — for free, without any jargon. You are doing one of the hardest jobs there is. You deserve the support that is there for you.
