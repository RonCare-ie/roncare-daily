Ireland Implements Highest-Ever Carer's Allowance Income Disregards from July 2, Boosting 2,700 Family Carers

<cite index="48-3,48-5,48-6">Major changes to Carer's Allowance came into effect on July 2, 2026, with the Department confirming the largest-ever increase to weekly income disregards—from €625 to €1,000 for single carers and €1,250 to €2,000 for couples</cite>.

Something significant happened for Irish carers this week. On 2 July 2026, the largest-ever increase to the Carer's Allowance income disregard came into effect — meaning thousands of family carers who were previously locked out of the payment can now qualify for the first time. Yet just days later, on 8 July, Family Carers Ireland filed its pre-budget submission calling for payments to rise even further, because the charity's State of Caring 2026 report found that 71% of Ireland's 624,000 family carers are struggling to make ends meet. It's a moment that captures exactly where carers in Ireland stand right now: more supported than ever on paper, but still carrying an enormous, often invisible load.

If you are caring full-time for a parent, partner, or child at home, the first thing to check right now is whether you qualify for Carer's Allowance — and the rules just got a lot more generous. From 2 July 2026, a single carer can earn up to €1,000 per week (after tax and deductions) and still qualify; for couples, the joint income disregard rose to €2,000 per week. The current weekly payment rate is €270 if you are under 66 and caring for one person, or €308 if you are 66 or over — and if you are caring for two or more people, that rises to €405 or €462 respectively. You can apply online through MyWelfare.ie, and Citizens Information has a fully updated guide on eligibility, the means test, and the capital rules — including the fact that the first €50,000 of your savings is completely ignored when assessing your finances.

On top of the weekly payment, every carer providing full-time care is entitled to the Carer's Support Grant — €2,000 per year, paid each June, and completely tax-free. It is not means-tested, so even if you earn too much to qualify for Carer's Allowance, you may still be entitled to the grant. If you already receive Carer's Allowance, Carer's Benefit, or Domiciliary Care Allowance, the €2,000 lands in your account automatically — no form needed. If you do not receive any of those payments, you can apply using form CSG1, available at gov.ie/csg.

If your loved one needs extra help at home — getting in and out of bed, washing, dressing, or preparing meals — the HSE Home Support Service (formerly called the Home Care Package) is also worth exploring. It is free, it is not means-tested, and you do not need a medical card to apply. It is open to people aged 65 and over, and also to younger people with conditions such as early-onset dementia or a disability. To apply, fill in the HSE Home Support Service application form, which you can download online or request from your local HSE Home Support Office. A GP, public health nurse, or other healthcare professional can also apply on behalf of your loved one. Once approved, a Care Needs Assessment is carried out and a personalised plan is put in place — you can even choose your own approved provider through the Consumer Directed Home Support option.

If you take one thing from this today, let it be this: go to citizensinformation.ie and spend ten minutes reading through the Carer's Allowance page — it was updated just last week to reflect the new July 2026 rules. You may find you now qualify for a payment you did not before, or that someone you know does. Caring for a loved one is hard enough without leaving money or support on the table that is rightfully yours.
