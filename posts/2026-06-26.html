Ireland's State of Caring 2026 Report Reveals 71% of Family Carers Struggling to Make Ends Meet with Severe Loneliness

Family Carers Ireland's landmark study shows carers cutting back on food and heating while 75% have never received respite care.

Ireland's 624,000 family carers are under mounting pressure, and a major new report has just put that reality in sharp focus. Family Carers Ireland launched its State of Caring 2026 report in early June, and the numbers are stark: 71% of carers are struggling to make ends meet, while 48% experience severe loneliness. Almost half of those in acute financial difficulty are cutting back on food and heating. This is the backdrop against which thousands of Irish families are quietly doing one of the hardest jobs imaginable — caring for a parent, child, or partner at home, often without enough support.

If you care full-time for a loved one and are wondering what you are entitled to, Carer's Allowance is the main weekly payment to know about. The current 2026 rate is €270 per week if you are under 66, or €308 per week if you are 66 or over. On top of that, the annual Carer's Support Grant — a separate, non-taxable lump sum — is €2,000 in 2026, paid automatically in June if you are already on Carer's Allowance or Carer's Benefit. If you care for more than one person, you can receive one and a half times the standard weekly rate.

One of the biggest changes landing this summer is a significant loosening of the means test. From 2 July 2026, a single carer can earn up to €1,000 per week from work and still qualify for Carer's Allowance — up from €625. For couples, the combined disregard doubles to €2,000 per week. When it comes to savings, the first €50,000 you hold in the bank (excluding your family home) is fully ignored in the means test, with a sliding scale applying to anything above that.

If you were turned down or had your payment reduced before July 2026, it might be time to apply again — more people will now qualify under the new rules. Ring Citizens Information on 0761 07 4000 before you apply; they can give you a realistic estimate of your entitlement. If the person you look after needs extra hands-on help at home, the HSE Home Support Service is worth applying for as soon as possible. It is completely free and is not means-tested — your income or savings make no difference to eligibility.

The HSE Home Support Service is available to people aged 65 and over, and also to younger people with conditions like early-onset dementia or a disability. You simply fill in the HSE Home Support application form, available from your local HSE Home Support Office, and a community nurse will visit to carry out a care needs assessment. The process can take weeks, so apply early — families who wait for a crisis often end up paying privately in the meantime.

If you are a family carer in Ireland and you are not sure what you are entitled to, the single best thing you can do today is call Family Carers Ireland's free national careline on 1800 24 07 24. They can walk you through Carer's Allowance, the Carer's Support Grant, the HSE Home Support Service, and whatever else your situation calls for. You do not have to figure this out alone — and the supports, imperfect as they still are, are real and available right now.
