Irish Family Carers Face Acute Financial Pressure as State of Caring 2026 Report Reveals 71% Struggle to Make Ends Meet

New research shows Irish carers skip meals and cut heat while providing 90+ hours of unpaid care weekly, with 75% never receiving formal respite support.

If you are caring for a parent, partner, or child at home in Ireland right now, you are one of an estimated 624,000 people doing exactly that — quietly, often without enough support, and frequently at real financial cost to yourself. The State of Caring 2026 report, published by Family Carers Ireland in June, found that 71% of family carers are struggling to make ends meet. On 2 July 2026, the Department of Social Protection increased the Carer's Allowance means test significantly — if you were turned down before or never applied, it is worth taking another look.

Carer's Allowance is the main weekly payment for people who have left work or cut their hours to care full-time for a loved one. From January 2026, the full rate is €270 per week if you are under 66, or €308 per week if you are 66 or over. If you care for two or more people, you can receive 50% on top of that — up to €405 or €462 per week.

The big change from 2 July 2026 is that the weekly income disregard jumped from €625 to €1,000 for a single carer, and from €1,250 to €2,000 for a couple. In plain terms, a two-adult household earning around €110,000 a year can now keep the full payment. If you were previously told you earned too much, that answer may well have changed. You can apply online through MyWelfare at mywelfare.ie, or ring Citizens Information on 0761 07 4000 for a free estimate of what you might get before you apply — have a payslip and bank statement handy when you call.

Alongside the weekly payment, there is a Carer's Support Grant worth €2,000 per year, paid automatically each June to anyone already receiving Carer's Allowance, Carer's Benefit, or Domiciliary Care Allowance. It is not means-tested — you do not have to declare your savings or income to get it — and it is completely tax-free. If you care for more than one person, you receive a separate €2,000 for each of them. If you are not on any of those payments, you can still apply using form CSG1 from the Department of Social Protection, as long as you provide full-time care for at least six months of the year.

The HSE Home Support Service provides personal care hours at home for older people and those with a disability, and it is completely free and not means-tested — you do not need a medical card or a certain income level to apply. You do need a formal care needs assessment, which an HSE public health nurse or occupational therapist carries out. You can apply by contacting your local HSE Home Support Office directly — find yours at hse.ie/homesupportservice — or by calling HSE Live free on 1800 700 700. Waiting lists vary by area, as this is a discretionary service rather than a legal entitlement.

Ring Citizens Information on 0761 07 4000 — it is free, confidential, and staffed by people who know this system inside out. They can tell you in one call whether you qualify for Carer's Allowance at the new July 2026 rates, point you towards the Carer's Support Grant, and help you start a Home Support application. You do not have to figure this out alone.
