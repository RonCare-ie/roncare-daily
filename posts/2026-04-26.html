Support & Community for Family Carers

Resources, guidance, and solidarity for those caring for a loved one across Europe

Across Ireland and the United Kingdom, the quiet crisis facing unpaid family carers has rarely felt more urgent. In Ireland alone, family carers provide approximately 19 million hours of unpaid care every week, saving the State an estimated €20 billion per year — a staggering contribution that President Síle de Búrca Connolly, speaking at the first ever National Young Carers Conference at UCD in March 2026, said remains "insufficiently recognised." That sense of undervaluation is not merely symbolic: Family Carers Ireland's own research shows that almost 70% of carers face financial hardship, nearly a third cutting back on essentials such as food and heating, while over 72% have never received any form of respite care. In the UK, meanwhile, tens of thousands of carers have been quietly carrying debt built up through no fault of their own, the result of confusing government guidance on earnings rules that stretched from 2015 to 2025. This week, a series of new developments — from a landmark UK government debt-relief exercise, to Ireland's improved payment thresholds, to the very first UK-wide Power of Attorney Day — has placed the lives of family carers firmly back in the spotlight.

For carers in the UK, a significant act of redress arrived this month. The British government launched a major reassessment exercise covering over 200,000 Carer's Allowance cases, with around 25,000 unpaid carers set to have debts reduced, cancelled entirely, or refunded where money had already been repaid. The debts arose because carers juggling paid work alongside at least 35 hours of unpaid caring built up overpayments without realising they had exceeded the weekly earnings limit — the result of government guidance that did not accurately reflect the law between April 2015 and September 2025. Crucially, carers do not need to take any action: the Department for Work and Pensions will contact those affected directly. The review follows the government's acceptance of 38 of 40 recommendations from the independent Sayce Review. Separately, from April 2026, Carer's Allowance itself has risen to £86.45 per week, while the weekly earnings threshold has increased to £204 net — meaning some unpaid carers can now earn around £10,000 a year and still receive the benefit. Carers UK's helpline (0808 808 7777, Monday to Friday, 9am–6pm) and their email service (advice@carersuk.org) remain available for anyone with questions about these changes.

In Ireland, Budget 2026 has delivered several meaningful, if incremental, improvements for carers. The maximum weekly rate of Carer's Allowance, Carer's Benefit, and Disability Allowance each increased by €10 from January 2026, while Domiciliary Care Allowance rose by €20 to €380 per month. From July 2026, the Carer's Allowance income disregard will increase significantly — to €1,000 per week for a single person and €2,000 for a couple — meaning many more families who were previously excluded due to means-testing may now qualify. The annual Carer's Support Grant for 2026 stands at €2,000, is tax-free, and will be paid automatically on 4 June to those already receiving Carer's Allowance, Carer's Benefit, or Domiciliary Care Allowance; those not receiving these payments can apply using form CSG1 from Citizens Information. Carers who are unsure whether these recent changes affect their entitlements are encouraged to ring Citizen Information on 0761 07 4000, who can give an estimate of any new rate or help check whether a previous means-test assessment was carried out correctly. Beyond finances, an RTÉ article published just two days ago (24 April 2026) highlighted that organisations such as Family Carers Ireland offer support services, carelines, and a free counselling service — and that peer support groups, both online and in person, can make a vital difference to the loneliness and social isolation that so many carers experience.

This week also brought a timely reminder of one of the most overlooked practical tools available to family carers. On 22 April 2026, Carers UK and Standard Life hosted the first ever UK-wide Power of Attorney Day — a national campaign encouraging all adults to plan ahead by putting a Power of Attorney (POA) in place before a crisis strikes. A Power of Attorney is a legal document that allows a person to appoint someone they trust to make decisions about their finances, property, or health and welfare if they lose capacity. For family carers, having this in place can provide clarity in emergencies and avoid stressful, often costly, legal hurdles at the very moment swift decisions are needed. Without one, families may face lengthy court applications to be appointed as a deputy or controller. Across the UK, this month has also been Stress Awareness Month, with carers' organisations marking the theme of #BeTheChange — Small Actions, Big Impact — specifically acknowledging the mental health toll on unpaid carers. Research from the University of Limerick found that initially healthy carers displayed a 33% greater risk of illness or disability eight years later, and Family Carers Ireland surveys found a 30% increase among carers diagnosed with depression over the past decade. These figures are sobering, but help is available: in the UK, the Carers UK helpline and Samaritans (116 123, 24 hours) offer immediate support, while in Ireland, the HSE provides free helplines and access to peer support groups online and in person.

If you are currently caring for a loved one and feel overwhelmed, you are not alone — and you do not have to wait for a crisis to seek help. The single most concrete step you can take today is to check what you are entitled to. In Ireland, ring Citizens Information on 0761 07 4000 to find out whether you qualify for Carer's Allowance, the Carer's Support Grant, or Carer's Benefit under the new, more generous July 2026 thresholds — many carers who were previously excluded by the means test may now qualify. In the UK, visit the Carers UK website or call 0808 808 7777 to confirm your updated Carer's Allowance entitlements and find out if you are among those eligible for the Carer's Allowance debt reassessment. And wherever you are, consider looking into Power of Attorney for the person you care for — it is one of the most valuable and underused protections available, and setting it up now, while there is still time, could save your family enormous stress in the future. You carry an extraordinary load. There is support out there — please reach out and claim it.
