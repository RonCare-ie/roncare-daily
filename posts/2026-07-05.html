Today's Briefing

Irish Family Carers Report 71% Struggling Financially Despite Government Praise and Budget Increases

Family Carers Ireland's latest State of Caring 2026 report reveals nearly 70% face financial hardship with over 72% never receiving respite care, exposing persistent crisis in home-based care support.

Right now, somewhere in Ireland, a woman in her early fifties is dropping her teenage son to school, rushing to her mother's house to get her washed and dressed, and then logging on to work — all before 9am. She is not unusual. She is one of over 500,000 people in Ireland quietly holding everything together as an unpaid family carer.

Caring is physical work — heavy, relentless, and often invisible. On average, Irish family carers provide 38.7 hours of care per week, with 29% providing over 43 hours. That is more than a full-time job, with no salary, no sick days, and no one covering for you when your own body gives out.

The physical and mental toll runs deep. Carers face real health challenges: back injuries, depression, anxiety, and exhaustion that compounds over months and years. Yet 72% of carers have never accessed respite care — meaning most people carrying this load have never once had a proper break. Without that support, the weight of worry and isolation keeps building.

None of this means caring is not also an act of love — of course it is. But love does not protect your back, or your sleep, or your sense of self. The carers who are struggling are not failing; they are people doing too much for too long without enough support. If you recognise yourself in any of this, that recognition matters. You are allowed to need help too.

If you are a carer — or someone who loves one — you do not have to figure it all out alone. RonCare (roncare.ie) is a care coordination app built specifically for families managing care at home in Ireland. It is designed to take some of the mental load off: keeping everyone in the loop, tracking appointments, and making the day-to-day a little less chaotic. Because carers deserve support that actually fits their lives.
