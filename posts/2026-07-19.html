Irish Family Carers Face 'Test of Human Endurance' as Financial Crisis Deepens, New Report Shows

The State of Caring 2026 report reveals 71% of Irish family carers struggling to make ends meet, with 48% experiencing severe loneliness and many cutting back on essentials.

Somewhere in Ireland right now, a woman in her fifties is dropping her teenagers to school, driving to her mother's house to help her wash and dress, and then heading into work — all before 9am. She is not unusual. She is one of over 500,000 people in Ireland quietly carrying a full-time caring role on top of everything else in their lives. And statistically, she is almost certainly doing it at a cost to her own health that nobody is talking about.

The numbers are stark. Nearly half of all Irish family carers — 48% — have been diagnosed with a mental health condition, including 35% treated for depression and 39% for anxiety. These conditions don't always exist before caring begins. Many carers develop them as a direct result of the relentlessness, isolation, and invisible weight of being responsible for someone else's wellbeing around the clock. Yet 72% of carers have never accessed respite care — meaning most are carrying all of this completely alone.

The physical toll runs just as deep. Two thirds of Irish family carers — 67% — have been diagnosed or treated for a physical health condition, and 40% have a back injury specifically linked to their caring role. The average carer provides 38.7 hours of care per week, with nearly a third providing 43 hours or more. That is more than a full-time job, done without pay, without sick leave, and often without acknowledgement of how hard it is on the body. When 44% of carers report experiencing physical or emotional harm as part of their caring role, this is not a footnote — it is a crisis hiding in plain sight.

What makes this particularly painful is how little support most carers know exists. Real entitlements are available: Carer's Allowance, the €2,000 annual Carer's Support Grant paid every June, a Free GP Visit Card, and Carer's Leave protecting your job for up to 104 weeks. Many carers are simply never told these exist. If you are caring for someone right now and have never looked into your entitlements, Citizens Information is the best place to start.
